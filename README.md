# Data-Driven Mobility Study 🚗📊

### Overview:

This project analyzes large-scale traffic data from Israel’s Road 6 to investigate how travel behavior changed before and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

Using Python-based data analysis and multiple traffic engineering methods, the study quantifies structural shifts in mobility patterns and provides evidence of long-term behavioral changes in transportation demand.

### Key Findings:

📈 Statistically significant increase in travel volume across Israel in the post-pandemic period

🕒 Shifts in daily travel patterns, indicating changes in how demand is distributed throughout the day

🔄 Evidence of persistent post-COVID mobility behavior changes, rather than a temporary disruption

Other findings were precisely described in the study (document attached).

### Data:

Source: Road 6 traffic data (large-scale, time-series dataset).
Includes: vehicle counts, timestamps, and segment-level traffic information.
Data spans: pre-COVID (2019) and post-COVID (2022) periods.

Note: Due to data privacy and licensing, raw data may not be publicly available.

### Methodology:

- Data cleaning and preprocessing of high-volume traffic records
- Time-series analysis comparing pre- and post-pandemic periods
- Evaluatation of observed changes
- Visualization of temporal travel patterns (daily distributions, peak shifts)
